Travel Experiences

Here I share some of my favorite travel experiences and moments from different destinations.

1. Sikkim

Sikkim, nestled in the northeastern Himalayas of India, captivated me with its pristine beauty and serene ambiance. The journey began with a scenic drive from Bagdogra to Gangtok, where the winding roads and lush landscapes set the perfect backdrop for our adventure. One of the highlights was visiting Tsomgo Lake, a stunning glacial lake surrounded by snow-capped peaks. The tranquility of the turquoise waters was mesmerizing. A visit to Nathula Pass, on the Indo-China border, offered breathtaking panoramic views and a sense of standing at a historic crossroad. Exploring the Rumtek Monastery was a spiritual experience, with its intricate architecture and peaceful surroundings. The aura of this revered Buddhist site was truly enchanting. In Pelling, the vistas of the Kanchenjunga range were awe-inspiring, and the sacred Khecheopalri Lake added a mystical touch to our journey. Sikkim's blend of natural splendor and rich cultural heritage left an indelible mark on me. The warmth of the locals, the serene monasteries, and the majestic mountains made this trip an unforgettable escape into a Himalayan paradise.

Travel Tips

Find useful tips and advice to make your travel easier and more enjoyable.

1. Sikkim

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March to May) and Autumn (September to November) are ideal. Avoid the monsoon season (June to August).
  • Permits: Foreign tourists need an Inner Line Permit (ILP) and a Protected Area Permit (PAP) for certain areas.
  • Altitude Sickness: Acclimatize gradually, stay hydrated, and carry medication like Diamox if prone to altitude sickness.
  • Packing Essentials: Layered clothing, warm jackets, raincoat, comfortable trekking shoes, sunglasses, sunscreen, and a basic first-aid kit.
  • Transportation: Local taxis and rental cars with drivers are the main modes of transportation. Negotiate fares in advance.
  • Local Cuisine: Try momos, thukpa, gundruk, and churpi. Explore local eateries for authentic flavors.
  • Respect Local Culture: Dress modestly and respect local customs, especially at religious sites. Ask permission before taking photos of people or inside monasteries.
  • Cash and ATMs: ATMs are available in towns like Gangtok and Pelling, but carry sufficient cash for remote areas.
  • Connectivity: Mobile network coverage can be spotty. BSNL and Airtel provide the best coverage. Inform family or friends of your travel plans in case of connectivity issues.
  • Environmental Responsibility: Avoid using plastic bags, dispose of waste properly, and respect the natural environment.
  • Local Guides: Hiring local guides can provide valuable insights into the culture, history, and hidden gems of Sikkim.
  • Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of important contacts such as your hotel, local travel agency, and emergency services.

Travel Itineraries

1. Itinerary for Sikkim

  • Day 1: Arrival in Gangtok: Arrive at Bagdogra Airport or NJP Railway Station and transfer to Gangtok. Check-in to your hotel and rest. In the evening, explore MG Marg for some local shopping and dining.
  • Day 2: Gangtok Sightseeing: Visit Tsomgo Lake and Baba Mandir. Enjoy the beautiful views and the serene atmosphere. Return to Gangtok and visit the Directorate of Handicrafts & Handloom and Enchey Monastery.
  • Day 3: Nathula Pass and Changu Lake: Take a trip to Nathula Pass (Indo-China border) and Changu Lake (also known as Tsomgo Lake). Make sure to carry warm clothing as it can get quite cold here.
  • Day 4: Gangtok to Pelling: Drive to Pelling. On the way, visit Ravangla and the Buddha Park. Check-in to your hotel in Pelling and rest for the night.
  • Day 5: Pelling Sightseeing: Explore the Khecheopalri Lake, Pemayangtse Monastery, Rabdentse Ruins, and the Singshore Bridge (the highest bridge in Sikkim). Enjoy the panoramic views of the Kanchenjunga range.
  • Day 6: Pelling to Yuksom: Drive to Yuksom, the historic town where the first Chogyal of Sikkim was crowned. Visit the Norbugang Coronation Throne, Dubdi Monastery, and the Kathok Lake.
  • Day 7: Return to Gangtok: Return to Gangtok. On the way, visit the Namchi Char Dham and Samdruptse Statue. Check-in to your hotel and spend the evening at leisure.
  • Day 8: Departure: Transfer to Bagdogra Airport or NJP Railway Station for your onward journey. Take back wonderful memories of your Sikkim trip.
